Comprehensive Waste Management Proposals

The following proposals aim to develop a strict system to ensure that every citizen is trained on waste management, waste workers get strict training and required safety gear, waste management facilities are present in every ULB/GP, and a dedicated decentralised monitoring system is in place.

  1. Mandatory training for every citizen

    Similar to countries where military training is mandatory for every citizen, in India, there shall be mandatory Waste Management training for every Waste Generator/Citizen. This will ensure source segregation, which will result in better waste management, lesser load on waste management facilities and landfills.

    1. Physical training on types of waste, source segregation, how to make compost at home, re-use of plastic, etc.
    2. Distribution of 3 types of dustbins for dry, wet and domestic hazardous waste to every household/citizen.
    3. Distribution of a designed and tested compost making kit to every household/citizen.
    4. App-based monitoring.

  2. Phase-wise training to all Waste Workers

    Professional training programs to ensure waste workers have proper knowledge and equipment to handle different types of waste safely and efficiently.

  3. Formation of Area Committee for Monitoring – "Green Champions"

    Decentralised approach for Monitoring has to be done at every step of Waste Management for bulk waste generators, residential areas, commercial & Public semi-public building, Industrial buildings, etc.

    1. Training of all Waste Generators
    2. Waste Generation: Source segregation happening or not, no wet waste to be collected
    3. Waste Collection happening on ground level
    4. Waste transportation
    5. Waste treatment
    6. Waste Disposal

  4. Incentive based approach

    Incentive to Bulk waste generators and other buildings for source segregation.

  5. Waste Movements in ULBs

    Taking example from Karnataka's Yadgir city, "If you see waste, send photo" similar movement to be made mandatory for every ULB regardless of its size and population, to ensure community participation.

  6. Community Participation

    One day of all working staff regardless of employment type to be engaged in Cleaning of Public areas, waste management. Inspired from Cleaning-day followed in schools every week, every waste generator to be responsible for keeping the country clean and contributing to waste management, starting from Govt. sector.

  7. Penalization system

    Fines to be imposed and no waste collection for buildings not segregating waste at source as punishment.

  8. Waste Management Facilities to be made available in every ULB

    1. Biomethanization plant
    2. W-to-E plant
    3. Multiple Recycling centres for different types of waste
    4. Scrap collection shops – Online app based

  9. Complete Digital App-based system for

    1. Training of all Waste Generators
    2. Shopping of Waste utilities – compost kits, dustbins, etc.
    3. Tracking of Waste collection vehicles
    4. Location of Waste management facilities, Recycling centres, scrap shops etc.
    5. Option to upload geo-tagged photos of dumping sites, etc.
